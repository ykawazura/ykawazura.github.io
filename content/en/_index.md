## About

I am an Associate Professor in the School of Data Science and Management at Utsunomiya University (since 2024). My research focuses on plasma physics and astrophysics — in particular, the turbulent dynamics of magnetized plasmas in astrophysical systems such as accretion flows and solar wind.

My work combines analytical theory, gyrokinetic and magnetohydrodynamic simulations, and high-performance computing to understand how turbulent energy is dissipated in collisionless and weakly collisional plasmas. I developed the pseudospectral MHD codes [Calliope](https://github.com/ykawazura/calliope) and [Gallope](https://github.com/ykawazura/gallope) and have run large-scale simulations on supercomputers including Fugaku (RIKEN).

Previously, I was an Assistant Professor at Tohoku University (2019–2024), a Postdoctoral Research Assistant at the University of Oxford (2016–2019), and an Assistant Professor at the University of Tokyo (2014–2016).
